Feeding a Reggie, and their diet

Overview

Reggies, despite their usual small stature and cute appearance, are aggressive eaters. While they descend from alligators, they have evolved into omnivores, yet retain their aggression when it comes to food. It is important to stay aware of this fact when feeding reggies.

Diet

It is important to know what a reggie can and can't actually digest for your health as well as theres. A reggie in its natural habitat will eat anything it comes across, inanimate or not. This makes it hard to distinguish what they normally eat, as it is inconsistent. When left alone, reggies have been observed to eat the following:

  • Cloth objects, such as clothes or blankets
  • Drywall and fiberglass insultation
  • Cleaning chemicals and detergent
  • Plastic objects and packaging

One important thing to note is that none of the above are considered edible. They will occasionally consume edible food, although this is purely by accident. Because reggies are descendants of alligators, they can go a long time inbetween meals without eatings. It is not recommended you let a reggie eat such objects, but it will happen.

Food Preferences and Allergies

Further complicating a reggies diet is their allergies. Being carnivores, they have evolved to subsist primarily on meat. However, this is at odds with their food preferences. While it's true that they eat anything and everything, they will eat some more than others.
