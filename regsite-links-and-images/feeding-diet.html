Feeding Reggi, what they can and can't eat

Reggis are hungry and will eat you out of house and home. That is, assuming Reggi doesn't literally eat it first!

A reggi enjoying his "food"

Diet

Reggis, despite their usual small stature and cute appearance, are aggressive eaters. While they descend from alligators, they have evolved into omnivores, yet retain their aggression when it comes to food. It is important to stay aware of this fact when feeding reggis.

Digestion

It is important to know what a reggi can and can't actually digest for your health as well as theres. A reggie in its natural habitat will eat anything it comes across, inanimate or not. This makes it hard to distinguish what they are actually able to digest

Objects Consumed

When left alone, reggis have been observed to eat the following:

Cloth objects, such as clothes or blankets
Drywall and fiberglass insultation
Cleaning chemicals and detergent
Plastic objects and packaging

One important thing to note is that none of the above are considered edible. They will occasionally consume edible food, although this is purely by accident. Because reggies are descendants of alligators, they can go a long time inbetween meals without eatings. It is not recommended you let a reggie eat such objects, but it will happen.

For specifics on what you should and should not feed reggi, see the section below.

Healthy Foods

While reggi may not like them, these foods are the healthiest options. You may need to convince (or trick) them into eating the following:

  • Carrots
  • Bread ( Crust cut off )
  • Corned beef
  • Potatoes ( Ideally mashed )
  • Cheerios
  • Apples ( Must be pre sliced )
  • Corn ( creamed )
  • Fish food
  • Shredded cheese ( From the bag )

Unhealthy Foods

These are reggi's favorite foods. By no means let him eat them. They will likely do so anyways when you leave for work.

  • Gummy worms ( or real worms )
  • Fiberglass insulation
  • Fruity pebbles
  • Dust bunnies
  • Spiders
  • Candle wax
  • Wires
  • Tide pods
  • Prescription medication

These represent the most commonly eaten items by domesticated reggis. We ideally would take the time to document every item, but there are too many items to reasonably cover in one article. We advise you exercise your best judgement regarding any items that have not been covered.
